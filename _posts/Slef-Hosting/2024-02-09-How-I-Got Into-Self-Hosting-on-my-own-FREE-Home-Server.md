It's been about 2–3 years since I stopped relying on many commercial services and started hosting my own stuff at home for free. And you know what? My home self-hosted services are often better than the fancy ones you pay for. Back then, I barely knew Linux and could only figure out how to set up port forwarding. But guess what? That's all you need to start out.

## Baby Steps
### My first server

So, being a total newbie, I didn't want to splash out on a fancy server. Plus, as a broke student, I had zero cash to spare. Instead, I scavenged for an old desktop. Luckily, a friend had one lying around, an ancient Dell Desktop with less than 2GBs of RAM and some old Pentium CPU. It was ancient, but hey, it worked!

After a good clean-up and some fresh paste, it was time to slap on an OS. I stumbled upon Open Media Vault and Docker containers in a YouTube tutorial. Looked easy enough, so I gave it a shot. I had no clue what Docker even was, but after some trial and error, I got the hang of it pretty quickly.

From there, I just tinkered around with whatever my old server could handle. The highlights were AdguardHome for blocking pesky ads and Plex for my media collection. Sure, it wasn't super fast, but it did the job.

## The Upgrade
### Night and day difference for cheap

After a few months, my old server couldn't keep up. No surprise there. So, I scoured for a better deal and found a decent desktop for 80 bucks. It had 4GB RAM and some i3 processor from a few years back. I also splurged on a 16GB RAM kit for 8 bucks. No-brainer at that price.

This time, I decided to try Proxmox. Bit of a learning curve, but totally worth it. It's like a fancy playground where you can run all sorts of stuff. I chose PVE because after a while, I realized that relying only on Docker was kinda limiting. So, I branched out and started hosting even more stuff, including:

- Home Assistant, #1 smart home platform, and let me tell you, it's addictive.
- Plex and some other cool media stuff.
- Oh, and a Minecraft server, because why not?
- many other stuff like a personal wiki, NAS, VPN...

Switching to Proxmox was a game-changer. Suddenly, I had a whole new world of possibilities at my fingertips. And that's how I rolled into the world of self-hosting, one step at a time. Still a lot to learn though.

## Future Plans

As for the future, well, the sky's the limit. When I do upgrade again, it'll be to proper server equipment. No more repurposed desktops—I'm talking about the real deal. And you can bet I'll be maxing out my network setup too. With faster speeds and more reliable connections, the possibilities are endless. Who knows? Maybe one day I'll even host my own website or create a cloud storage solution. The world of self-hosting is vast, and I can't wait to see where it takes me.
